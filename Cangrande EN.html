CANGRANDE

Casa di Romeo

The building next to the Scaliger Tombs is a well-preserved medieval house with a spacious inner courtyard and a tall crenellated wall protecting the entrance from the public street. Inside, an original portico is still visible on the front wall, while other sides have been modified over time. The staircase to the upper floors features Romanesque, Gothic, and Renaissance windows, topped by original crenellations. During the 13th century, Verona was involved in conflicts between the Guelphs and Ghibellines, and traces of this history are visible in the structure, such as the V-shaped crenellation on Romeo's house tower. The Montecchi used the symbol "a coda di rondine" to express their Ghibelline political affiliation. The "stallo delle Arche," once owned by the Nogarola in the 14th century, is famous for hosting Bailardino's will in 1339. After being part of the Scaliger family, the palace was later bought by the Bevilacqua in the 15th century and subsequently divided among different families, eventually becoming a "stallo." Currently inhabited as a private residence, it is not open to visitors, but its 14th-century Gothic façade perfectly suits Shakespeare's love story, with a commemorative inscription of the protagonists, Romeo and Juliet.

Arche Scaligere

"One of the most distinguished and significant monuments of Gothic art," according to the French historian Georges Duby, the Scaliger Arches are located in the heart of the historic center of Verona and constitute the monumental funerary complex of the Della Scala family, intended to contain the tombs (or arches) of the main representatives of the dynasty. Situated next to the Church of Santa Maria Antica, a few steps from Piazza dei Signori, they were built in the 14th century by various sculptors and also contain, among others, the tomb of the most famous of the Della Scala, Cangrande | (Can Francesco della Scala), lord of Verona from 1308 to 1311, skilled conqueror and cunning politician, precise administrator and generous patron, also known as a friend and protector of the Supreme Poet Dante Alighieri, who dedicated his Paradiso to him. The tombs are enclosed by a wrought-iron fence where we can notice the recurring motif of the ladder, symbol of the dynasty, while the sarcophagi are located on the ground or on raised platforms. The three main arches are: The Ark of Cangrande: A structure erected on the entrance door of the Church of Santa Maria Antica, with a sarcophagus supported by three dogs and bearing the banner of Cangrande. The statue depicts Cangrande lying down, with curial clothing and a broadsword beside him. On the tomb are engraved the cities of his kingdom and there is a copy of his equestrian statue, while the original is kept in the Museum of Castelvecchio. The Ark of Mastiff Il: Built during the life of Mastiff Il, is the most valuable. It has a quadrangular base and in the tympanums of the second order tabernacle there are four high reliefs with scenes of sacred history related to human sin. The sarcophagus of Mastino della Scala is depicted with the mastiff himself lying on the lid, with a serene expression, while below he is shown kneeling and supplicating before the Virgin Mary, invoking the words of the funeral inscription expressing the wish that Mastino's spirit may reach heaven and enjoy eternal peace. Above the ark, the equestrian statue of Mastino stands solemnly. The Ark of Cansignorio della Scala is conceived as a Gothic reliquary and was commissioned by Cansignorio himself while still alive, considering his serious health conditions. Designed by Bonino da Campione with the collaboration of various craftsmen, including local sculptors, the ark cost more than 10,000 florins, a significant amount. It features a hexagonal base with pillars supporting six Gothic tabernacles representing six warrior saints. The work was admired by many Romantic artists, who praised its beauty and charm. On the sarcophagus, stories from the Gospels are depicted along with an inscription attributed to Cansignorio himself, expressing his desire for rest in the ark and claiming his deeds as monarch of many Italian cities, emphasizing his valor, love for peace, and faith .

BLOG POSTS

  • Romeo and Juliet
    The myth of Romeo and Juliet is celebrated in theatrical performances and revisitations during the whole year. The most famous event is the Shakespearean Festival which takes place every summer at the Roman Theatre, second in Europe after the one in Stratford on Avon. Among the tourist reception initiatives most appreciated by visitors Welcome to Verona. Romeo and Juliet a love story: the most beautiful scenes of the drama Shakespearean are recited during the summer, in Italian and English, in their ideal setting, the courtyard and Juliet's balcony, where legend has it that the two lovers really met and loved each other.
  • Nativity scenes and traditions
    The exhibition is held for the entire duration of the Christmas holidays International of Nativity scenes in Art and Tradition, set up in the arches of the Arena. The exhibition hosts around 400 specimens from the most important museums every year international and private collections. The installation is made with evocative special effects and offers a unique show, which attracts dozens of people every year thousands of visitors. Tocatì, International Street Games Festival During the Christmas period, Piazza Bra also hosts the Comet, a large one metal arch-sculptures by architect Rinaldo Olivieri, which emerge from the centre of the Arena, drawing a spectacular luminous arch seventy meters high. Designed for the inauguration of the first international exhibition of nativity scenes, the Comet Star is which has become the most famous symbol of Christmas in Verona. The Tocatì festival has also fully entered the Veronese traditions international street games, held at the end of September in the historic centre of the city: for three days the cars disappear to make room for dozens of games traditional, involving adults and children. Every year Tocatì welcomes a nation guest of honor, who presents his culture and traditions through spectacular performances.
